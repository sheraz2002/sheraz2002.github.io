AI for Medicine Specialization

deeplearning.ai · Coursera

Certificate: View Credential

Overview: A 3-course program on applying modern machine learning to healthcare: medical diagnosis (imaging & NLP), prognosis (risk modeling & survival analysis), and treatment (effect estimation & decision support). Focus on clinical metrics, dataset shift, bias, and building trustworthy models.

Duration: ~2–3 months (self-paced)

Course-by-Course Highlights

  • AI for Medical Diagnosis: Convolutional networks for chest X-rays and fundus images; multi-label classification; class imbalance handling; reading curves (ROC/PR), specificity/sensitivity operating points; calibration and thresholding.
  • AI for Medical Prognosis: Risk prediction from tabular EHR/time-series; missing-data strategies; survival analysis (Kaplan–Meier, Cox proportional hazards); evaluation with C-index and time-dependent metrics.
  • AI for Medical Treatment: Treatment effect estimation from observational data, propensity scores & matching intuition, off-policy evaluation, decision support; pitfalls such as confounding and dataset shift.

Mini-Projects & Exercises

  • Trained a CNN to detect multi-label pathologies on chest X-rays; tuned operating thresholds for clinical use cases with fixed specificity.
  • Built a prognosis model from longitudinal vitals and labs, compared Cox vs. gradient-boosted approaches using C-index.
  • Estimated individualized treatment effects (ITE) with simple uplift modeling and checked robustness against confounding signals.

Why it matters: Healthcare ML needs more than accuracy. This specialization strengthened my ability to choose clinically meaningful metrics, handle imbalance and shift, communicate uncertainty, and design safer decision-support systems.
