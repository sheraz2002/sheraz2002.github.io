Intern (Semiconductor Chip Design)

National Institute of Electronics (NIE)

Duration: Jul 2023 – Aug 2023

Summary: Hands-on VLSI/analog-mixed-signal design using Cadence Virtuoso and Spectre—covering schematic capture, simulation, layout, and verification for building-block IP such as amplifiers, comparators, and ADCs. Worked end-to-end with PDK devices, from biasing/topology selection to layout practices and sign-off checks.

  • Designed and simulated analog building blocks: two-stage and folded-cascode op-amps, differential pairs, current mirrors, band-gap style biasing, and dynamic/static comparators in Cadence Virtuoso ADE (DC/AC/Transient).
  • Built a proof-of-concept SAR-ADC sub-system: sample-and-hold, dynamic comparator, and capacitor-DAC array; evaluated INL/DNL, ENOB, decision time, and reference settling through Spectre sweeps.
  • Performed stability checks on amplifiers (gain/phase margin, GBW, SR), and analyzed noise (thermal/1-f) and offset for precision blocks.
  • Completed layout for key cells using Virtuoso Layout: common-centroid/interdigitated placement for matched devices, guard-rings, shielding, well ties, and careful routing to minimize parasitics and crosstalk.
  • Followed good analog practices: device sizing for overdrive and headroom, bias networks, matching areas, thermometer vs. binary switching in DAC arrays, and clocking for SAR control.

Tools & Flow: Cadence Virtuoso (Schematic/Layout), Spectre/ADE for simulation, PDK-provided devices and models. Documentation of results and design choices for hand-off and review.

The internship strengthened my fundamentals in analog/mixed-signal IC design and gave me a complete view of the flow—from topology selection and simulation all the way to layout and sign-off checks— particularly for amplifiers and ADC building blocks.
