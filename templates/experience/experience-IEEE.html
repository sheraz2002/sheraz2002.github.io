Co-Head Robotics & Automation

IEEE PIEAS Student Branch

Duration: Sep 2022 – Jun 2024 (two consecutive terms)

Summary: Led the Robotics & Automation vertical at IEEE PIEAS SB—running hands-on training camps, designing competitions, and building a learning pipeline that took students from fundamentals to deployable robots and embedded systems.

  • Organized and delivered training camps on robotics fundamentals, control, and embedded programming (C/C++ on Arduino/STM32/ESP32), covering digital I/O, timers, PWM, UART/SPI/I²C, sensors, and motor drivers.
  • Co-designed and managed flagship events Roboriot and Automatex—wrote problem statements and rules, created judging rubrics, prepared arenas, published build guides, and led the on-site technical & logistics teams.
  • Mentored student teams end-to-end: requirement breakdown, mechanism selection, electronics wiring, firmware architecture, and PID/trajectory tuning for line-following, maze-solving, and task-automation challenges.
  • Built starter kits and curated BOMs for workshops; standardized controller stacks (Arduino/STM32, PlatformIO, STM32CubeIDE) and simulation tools (Proteus/Multisim, basic ROS2/CoppeliaSim intros).
  • Established lab practices—hardware inventory, check-in/out, ESD & safety SOPs—and a lightweight template repository with sample drivers and code snippets to accelerate team onboarding.
  • Coordinated with faculty, sponsors, and partner societies for venues, equipment, and prizes; handled scheduling, volunteer assignments, and event briefings to ensure smooth execution.
  • Drove outreach and documentation: posters, how-to sheets, wiring diagrams, and recap posts to sustain community momentum and help newcomers ramp quickly.
  • Outcomes across both terms (Sep 2022–Oct 2023 and Sep 2023–Jun 2024): consistent participation growth, stronger project quality, and a self-sustaining pipeline of mentors for subsequent cohorts.

These terms strengthened my leadership in technical communities and sharpened my ability to turn complex topics into practical, build-ready learning experiences—skills I now apply to real-world embedded, robotics, and AI projects.
