Lab Assistant

PIEAS Lab

Duration: 6 months

Responsibilities: As a Lab Assistant in the Microprocessing and Interfacing Lab at PIEAS, I supported students in understanding key embedded systems concepts. My role involved:

  • Teaching assembly language programming for STM32 microcontrollers, focusing on low-level hardware control.
  • Guiding students on STM32 and ESP32 architectures, including practical coding in STM32CubeIDE for timers, ADCs, and GPIO operations.
  • Explaining sensor interfacing techniques, helping students connect and program various sensors (e.g., temperature, proximity) with microcontrollers.
  • Introducing C programming and the Keil IDE for embedded development, covering basics like variable declarations, loops, and interrupts.
  • Using the STM32 Blue Pill board for small-scale projects to enhance student understanding, such as LED blinking, motor control, and basic PWM applications.

This role allowed me to develop strong mentoring skills while deepening my expertise in embedded systems education.
