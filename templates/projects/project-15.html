Class-AB Audio Amplifier (4 Ω, 10 W)

±100 mV Input · Two CE Preamps · Class-AB Push-Pull Output · TIP31C/TIP32C · LTspice

Designed a three-stage audio amplifier to drive a 4 Ω, 10 W speaker from a small ±100 mV input. The architecture uses two common-emitter preamplifier stages for voltage gain and a Class-AB output stage for current drive and efficiency.

Highlights: 4 Ω · 10 W ±100 mV → ≈10 V Two CE stages Class-AB output TIP31C/TIP32C LTspice simulation

Project Overview

Target was to reach ~10 V output swing and several amps of peak current into 4 Ω. The design splits into a low-noise front end (voltage amplification) and a robust push-pull output (current amplification) with thermal-friendly biasing.

Block Diagram

Stage 1 — CE Preamplifier

  • Input: ±100 mV
  • Measured gain: ≈22
  • Output: ≈2.2 V
  • Notes: Designed for higher gain initially, then tuned for stability and headroom.

Stage 2 — CE Preamplifier

  • Input: ≈2.2 V
  • Gain: ≈4.2
  • Output: ≈9.4 V
  • Total front-end gain: ≈92×

Output Stage — Class-AB Push-Pull

  • Topology: Complementary Class-AB with bias network.
  • Devices: 3× TIP31C (NPN) + 1× TIP32C (PNP).
  • Supply: Raised from ~11 V to ~13 V to improve drive and symmetry.
  • Current gain: ~15 mA → ~2.5 A peak capability.

Design Notes

  • Biasing & Thermal: Class-AB bias set to minimize crossover distortion while limiting idle dissipation.
  • Headroom: Front-end gain chosen to avoid clipping under typical supply and load variations.
  • Device Matching: Discrete complementary pairs can introduce mismatch that impacts efficiency.

Conclusion

The three-stage topology achieves the required voltage gain and current drive for a 4 Ω, 10 W speaker from a ±100 mV input. Using a Class-AB push-pull output with Darlington assistance provides strong current delivery with manageable distortion and heat.
